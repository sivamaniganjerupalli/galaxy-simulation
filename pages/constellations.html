Mythology & Legends

Long before modern astronomy, civilizations connected stars into legendary figures, mythical creatures, heroes and celestial guardians.

Greek Mythology

Orion

Orion was a mighty hunter in Greek mythology and remains one of the most recognizable constellations visible from Earth.

Celestial Bear

Ursa Major

Represents the Great Bear and contains the famous Big Dipper asterism used for navigation throughout history.

Royal Constellation

Cassiopeia

A queen in Greek mythology, famous for its distinctive W-shaped pattern visible throughout much of the year.

Understanding Constellations

Constellations are officially defined regions of the sky rather than simply groups of stars. Modern astronomers divide the celestial sphere into 88 recognized constellations, helping observers locate stars, galaxies, nebulae and other deep-space objects.

88 Regions

The sky is divided into 88 official constellation boundaries.

Navigation

Ancient sailors used constellations to navigate across oceans.

Deep Space

Many galaxies and nebulae are identified by the constellation they appear in.

Seasonal Sky
